What is DFARS Compliance?

In 2015, in the aftermath of data breaches at the Office of Personnel Management, a breach that impacted the personally identifiable information of over 21.5 million government employees and contractors, the Department of Defense decided to implement new regulations in order to safeguard the information systems of government contractors from cyber threats.

In particular, controlled technical information that is processed, stored, or transmitted through contractor’s information technology (IT) infrastructure must be protected. Contractors handling CTI must comply with the rules governing their information system security. These include DFARS 252.204-7012, DFARS 252.239-7010 and NIST SP 800-171. Failure to comply with these sections may result in loss of contracts, fines, and the inability to secure government contracts in the future.
